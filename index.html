Climate change and it's effects on Ethiopia

How Is Life In Ethiopia and Challanges

For the second year in a row Ethiopia ranks as number 2 on watchlist. That makes it one of the top countries most at risk of worsening Humanitarian needs in 2023. According to the United Nation 20 Million people in Ethiopia are facing food insecurity which means they don't have access to basic food and health. Where people are starving to death each day. A lot is going on right now in Ethiopia and all these problems make people's lives harder than it needs to be.Moreover, the episodic fighting between the Ethiopian government and Tigray’s people on the northside is not helping the situation at all. On top of that inflation is higher than ever and it makes things harder than before. In addition, drought has also impacted on people's access to food, especially those who rely on agriculture for living.

Climate Change Effects

Furthermore, climate change has affected the on people’s life in Ethiopia dramatically. Ethiopia is heading towards the 6th back to back failed rainy season. This has already affected 30 million people and it's only going to get worse if the drought continues. Climate change has affected people with farms and life stocks dramatically. Some people lost up to 90% of their life stock due to this. Others with farms are struggling to provide food and basic human needs for their families.

How Its Effecting The People

With all being said, these economic issues , government and people conflicts, and climate changes, only made civilian lives harder. Nevertheless, they made people consider leaving their homeland. Because of this crisis some people decided to move to bigger cities or even risk their and families' whole life and travel to a different country to simply survive and have a better life.
